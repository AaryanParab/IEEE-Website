Instructions

Prerequisites : None

If you want to take your programming to next level, here's the opportunity so grab it!

  • The Coder's Week is a free challenge in which anyone can participate, i.e he need not be an IEEE member to participate.
  • Participants can choose as many tracks as they want.
  • The programming challenge is of 7 days (6 days of learning & the 7th day for project allocation).
  • Each day, you will be provided with resources like documents, links to other websites, links to github repositories, links to videos, etc.
  • Tasks will be single/multiple depending on resources and topics provided. Participants have to maintain a GitHub Repository for each topic and tasks have to be updated in the repository within 24 hours.
  • After the end of 6 days, the status of the tasks of all candidates will be checked by the organising committee.
  • 2 life rule - You start with 2 lives. Each time you fail to complete a task within the 24-hour deadline, you lose a life. You must then complete the remaining tasks before 7th June. If you exhaust both lives and fail to complete the tasks for yet another day (i.e. fail to complete tasks for 3 days) you will be disqualified.
  • On 7th June, you will be provided with 3 project ideas from which you have to select any one and complete it within 3 days, i.e., by 10th June. Only Project of Participants who have completed the tasks of all 6 days will be considered (Undertaking a project is optional)
  • The Projects will be judged by a Young Professional from IEEE Computer Society based on certain criteria like amount of creativity, originality & features implemented. A Young Professional is an IEEE Member who is currently working in the field and has a wealth of information, knowledge and experience.

Rewards

Those who complete tasks for all 6 days will receive a Certificate of Participation.

Those who complete tasks for all 6 days as well as the project will receive a Certificate of Completion and Goodies from IEEE.

Those who completes tasks for all 6 days and is adjudged the best project will a receive Certificate of Completion, Goodies from IEEE and a chance to be mentored by a Young Professional of IEEE CS.

About Us

IEEE APSIT Student Chapter is a student body representing A.P Shah Institute of Technology on IEEE platform. We formed this committee of IEEE members to educate and to bring awareness of the latest technology prevailing in today's world. We strive to improve the quality of knowledge being imparted to students by organizing technical workshops and events which are led by motivational speakers. Our aim is to help students achieve a successful career by making them eligible for the current world of technology.
